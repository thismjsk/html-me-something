Poems About Pizza

This is exactly what is sounds like.

A wise man once said, "Poetry is a lot like pizza. It's a canvas in which one works true art."

On a foggy November afternoon, a close friend of mine thought aloud, "I have yet to be awestruck by the celebration of pizza conveyed through poetry. I nodded, and thought...could I embrace this challenge and convey pizza's majesty?

I shall do my best.

By: Jonathan McHambles

Pepperoni & Sausage

There's the first slice
lifted from the steam
the cheese ascending
pulled taught and stretched
until the snap of mozzarella
releases the marvel
to fulfill its destiny
marrying beef with pork
to throw a party in my belly.

The Delivery

I've waited
and waited
waited for you.

I'll keep waiting
and waiting
waiting for you.

I'm still waiting
just waiting
I'll wait forever, for you.

Someday you'll come
to help me believe, again
I believe you'll come.

Will you come
before my last breath
escapes my skull?

Should you come
after I'm gone
I'll wait for you.

I'll wait and wait
and wait
and wait...

[The narrator dies...]

[...ding-dong]
